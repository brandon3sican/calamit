MULAT-BLISTT Research Program

Monitored by DOST-PCIEERD and funded by DOST.

The research program was designed to produce up-to-date, high resolution information on Rainfall-Induced Landslides (RIL) in order to lower and prevent risks and losses in lives, livelihoods, health, community assets, and environmental quality. It was also intended to provide information for policies for early warning, evacuation, and development planning. Its goals are to support disaster mitigation and to strengthen disaster resilience.

  • Project 1: Modelling Susceptibility and Generating Rainfall-Threshold for Rainfall-Induced Landslide
  • Project 2: CALAMI-T - Cordillera Administrative LAndslide Monitoring Integrated Toolbox

The MULAT-BLISTT research program is a two-part collaboration of the the (UP-Baguio) University of the Philippines Baguio-(MGB-CAR) Mines and Geosciences Bureau-CAR, (UC) University of the Cordilleras-(UB) University of Baguio, (DOST-CAR) Department of Science and Technology - Cordillera Administrative Region, and (DOST-PCIEERD) Department of Science and Technology Philippine Council for Industry, Energy, Emerging Technology Research & Development.

Project 1

Modelling Susceptibility and Generating Rainfall-Threshold for Rainfall-Induced Landslide

One of the most exposed areas to rainfall-induced landslides is the BLISTT area of the Cordillera Administrative Region, which experienced nine out of ten of the country's greatest 24-hour rainfall totals during tropical cyclones.

The MULAT-BLISTT, which stands for Modelling and Understanding Landslide Events in BLISTT (Baguio, La Trinidad, Itogon, Sablan, Tuba, Tublay), is a research program funded by the DOST-PCIEERD. The program's objective is to increase disaster resilience in accordance with R.A. 10121, the "Philippine Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Act of 2010," and the UN 2030 Sustainable Development Goal.

Project 2

CALaMI-T - Cordillera Administrative Landslide Monitoring Integrated Toolbox

Project CALaMI-T is a landslide mitigation researc and development for BLISTT. It aims to develop a landslide movement model toolbox that can predict landslides and provide information on the impact of rain-induced landslides on communities, infrastructure, and the environment.

It is the Project 2 under the Program MULAT BLISTT: Modelling and Understanding LAndslide evenTs in Baguio-La Trinidad-Itogon-Sablan-Tuba-Tublay.
